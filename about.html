The Origin

How I Became a Men's Heartbreak Recovery Coach

I grew up in India, around meditation, discipline, and spiritual practice. My mother was a yogini. I was introduced to meditation when I was 5 or 6 years old. I went through formal practices like Transcendental Meditation early in life.

So on paper, I had everything. Awareness. Tools. Understanding.

But none of that stopped me from losing myself in love.

When I entered relationships, I didn't just love. I attached. I made my partner the center of my world. I gave more than I received. I adjusted myself to keep the connection. I told myself it was love.

But over time, something started fading. The attraction. The polarity. The sense of self.

And when the relationship ended, it didn't just hurt. It collapsed me.

Because I realized something I couldn't ignore:

I didn't just lose her.
I had lost myself inside the relationship.

That's where the real work began. Not in books. Not in ideas.

In my body. In my patterns. In my nervous system.

I had to sit with the grief instead of escaping it. See my codependency clearly. Rebuild my sense of self from the ground up.

That process changed everything.
